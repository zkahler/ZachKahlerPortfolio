Music That Inspires Me

Release Date: October 10, 1995

About the Album: Insomniac is Green Day's fourth studio album. Recorded at Hyde Street Studios in San Francisco, it features a heavier sound compared to "Dookie". The lyrics explore themes like alienation, anxiety, and drug use. Despite its darker tone, it was certified 2x Platinum.

Fun Fact: Insomniac was initially going to be titled "Jesus Christ Supermarket," but the band decided to change it. Billie Joe Armstrong has expressed regret over the change.

Release Date: January 28, 2013

About the Album: Opposites is the sixth studio album by Scottish alternative rock band Biffy Clyro. It is a double album, originally announced as two separate albums titled The Land at the End of Our Toes and The Sand at the Core of Our Bones, which later became the names of the individual discs.

Fun Fact: The album debuted at number one on the UK Albums Chart, making it Biffy Clyro's first number one album.

Release Date: June 24, 2022

About the Album: Vaxis - Act II: A Window of the Waking Mind is the tenth studio album by Coheed and Cambria. Like most of their albums, it's a concept album that continues the Amory Wars storyline.

Fun Fact: The album features a song called "The Liars Club", which was inspired by the murder of George Floyd and the subsequent social justice movements. The song explores themes of truth, deception, and the impact of such events on society.

Release Date: May 20, 1997

About the Album: The Colour and the Shape is the second studio album by the Foo Fighters, marking the first time the band recorded as a full group.

Fun Fact: The album title came from an inside joke about the band's tour manager's quirky shopping habits. He would buy random items just because he liked their color and shape, which inspired the name.

Release Date: January 8, 2013

About the Album: Vessel is the third studio album by Twenty One Pilots and their first release under the label Fueled by Ramen. It features a mix of genres including alternative hip hop, electropop, indie pop, rap rock, and electronica.

Fun Fact: Vessel is notable for having all of its tracks certified at least Gold by the RIAA, making Twenty One Pilots the first group or artist to achieve this feat with two separate albums. The album cover features the paternal grandfathers of band members Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun.

Release Date: March 6, 2020

About the Album: "You'll Be Fine" is the second studio album by the American emo band Hot Mulligan. It was released through No Sleep Records and features a mix of emo and pop punk styles.

Fun Fact: Did you know that the album's title, "You'll Be Fine," is stylized in all lowercase letters? It's a small but unique detail that adds to the band's quirky style.

Release Date: May 6, 2003

About the Album: This is the debut studio album by American rock band Fall Out Boy. It was released through the independent label Fueled by Ramen. The album features a mix of pop-punk and emo genres.

Fun Fact: The album's title comes from a line in the song "The Patron Saint of Liars and Fakes"? The line goes, "Take this to your grave, and I'll take it to mine." Also, the band recorded the album in a bit of a rush, living on a stranger's floor and running out of money halfway through the recording process!

Release Date: March 30, 1999

About the Album: "Shoot the Moon" is the fourth EP by the American punk rock band Pinhead Gunpowder. It was recorded in October 1998 in Billie Joe Armstrong's basement in Berkeley, California, and produced by Billie Joe Armstrong himself. The EP features seven tracks with a total length of 11:57.

Fun Fact: The EP was re-released on vinyl in 2022 by 1, 2, 3, 4 Go! Records, which included two additional tracks, "Sudsing Agents" and "El Lasso Grappo."

Release Date: April 1, 2003

About the Album: "Elephant" is the fourth studio album by the American rock duo The White Stripes. It was produced by the band's guitarist and lead vocalist Jack White1. The album continues their "back-to-basics" approach seen in their previous album.

Fun Fact: Jack White produced the album using antiquated equipment, including an eight-track tape machine and various gear no more recent than 1963. He did not use computers during the writing, recording, or production of the album.
